## Formatting and Writing Standards

The articles in this repository adhere to the **MIT academic writing guidelines**, including:

1. **Laboratory and Research Fundamentals** – structured according to [MIT 20.109 Laboratory Fundamentals in Biological Engineering Writing Guidelines](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/20-109-laboratory-fundamentals-in-biological-engineering-spring-2010/pages/assignments/guidelines-for-writing-up-your-research/), emphasizing:

   * Clear hypothesis and objectives
   * Systematic presentation of methods
   * Detailed results and discussions
   * Proper referencing and acknowledgment

2. **MIT Course 21 Writing Standards** – inspired by [MIT Course 21 Guide for Research Articles](https://www.mit.edu/course/21/21.guide/art-resc.htm), ensuring:

   * Coherent and logical structure
   * Precision in data presentation
   * Professional style and technical accuracy

3. **Practical Writing Insights from MIT Admissions** – using [MIT Admissions Guide to Academic Writing](https://mitadmissions.org/blogs/entry/how-i-write-academic-papers/) for enhancing readability and scholarly expression.

4. **Journal Article Guidelines** – following [MIT Journal Article Guidelines](http://web.mit.edu/2.tha/www/JournalArticleGuidelines.htm) to maintain standard formatting, proper figure and table integration, and high-quality scientific communication.
